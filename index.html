Help Guide

Job Orders Tab

All employer job requests live here. Each card shows the position, employer, hiring progress, and candidate pipeline. Click a job order to expand it and see each candidate's status, follow-up dates, and disposition stage.

Pipeline Funnel

Inside each expanded job order, the pipeline funnel shows how many candidates are at each stage: Broadcasted → Confirmed Interested → For Employer Review → Interview Scheduled → Hired. Use this to spot bottlenecks — if many are broadcasted but none are confirmed, follow up.

Broadcast Button

Sends the job details to a set of matching candidates. Open the Broadcast modal, use filters (job type, age, ERA score, gender, location) to narrow the list, select candidates, then click "Broadcast Selected." This creates a placement record at the Broadcasted stage for each one.

+ Add Button (Job Orders)

Manually adds a specific candidate to a job order when you already know who to place. Fill in their name or ID, set an initial status, and save. Use this for direct referrals or AI Matcher suggestions.

Candidates Tab

Your full pool of registered candidates. Use the filter bar to search by job type, location, ERA score, age range, gender, or setup preference. Click any candidate card to open their full profile — contact details, past jobs, notes, and ratings history.

+ Add to Job Order (from Candidates)

On any candidate card, click "+ Add to Job Order" to assign them directly to an open job order. A modal appears where you select the job order and set the initial response and status.

Screening Tab

Work through unscreened candidates systematically. Move each candidate through four stages: Unscreened → Contacted → In Progress → Ready for Matching. Candidates marked Ready appear as priority matches in the AI Matcher.

ERA Score

ERA stands for Eligibility, Responsiveness, and Attitude. Score each candidate across three pillars as you screen them. Higher ERA scores (7–10) indicate candidates who are document-verified, reply quickly, and have strong behavioral records — prioritize them for placements.
